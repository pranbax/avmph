Who are we?

AVM Philippines was formed and is being managed by a group of survivors of AVM with a primary purpose of helping fellow AVM patients and to raise awareness about this fatal condition.

A person doesn't really know he/she has AVM until it's too late.

What is AVM?

AVM stands for ArterioVenous Malformation.
Normally, arteries carry blood containing oxygen from the heart to the brain, and veins carry blood with less oxygen away from the brain and back to the heart. When an arteriovenous malformation (AVM) occurs, a tangle of blood vessels in the brain bypasses normal brain tissue and directly diverts blood from the arteries to the veins.

True AVM

This is the most common brain vascular malformation. It consists of a tangle of abnormal vessels connecting arteries and veins with no normal intervening brain tissue.

Cavernous Malformation

(also called a cavernoma, cavernous angioma, or a “cav-mal”).
This is a vascular malformation in the brain that doesn’t actively divert larrge amounts of blood. It may bleed and often produce seizures. This is a rare type of vascular malformation, meaning an abnormality of the blood vessels. A cav-mal can occur in any part of the body, but it's usually only a threat in the brain or spinal cord.

Venous Malformation

Venous malformations (VMs) are a type of type of vascular malformation that results from veins that have developed abnormally, which stretch or enlarge over time. VMs can be extremely painful and sensitive.

Dural Fistula

The covering of the brain is called the “dura mater.” An abnormal connection between blood vessels that involve only this covering is called a dural fistula. Dural fistulas can occur in any part of the brain covering.

Treatment Procedure

AVM treatment depends on different factors such as location and severity. Treatment for AVM are listed below.

Diagnostic Procedures:

  • Cerbral angiography - this test uses a special dye called a contrast agent injected into an artery. The dye highlights the structure of blood vessels to better show them on X-rays.

(cont.) Diagnostic Procedures:

  • Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) - uses powerful magnets and radio waves to show detailed images of the tissues. An MRI can pick up on small changes in these tissues.

  • Computerized tomography (CT) - CT scans use X-rays to create images of the head, brain or spinal cord and can help show bleeding.

Treatment Procedures:

  • Surgical removal (resection). If the brain AVM has bled or is in an area that can easily be reached, surgical removal of the AVM via conventional brain surgery may be recommended. In this procedure, your neurosurgeon removes part of your skull temporarily to gain access to the AVM.

Treatment Procedures:

  • Endovascular embolization. In this procedure, your doctor inserts a long, thin tube (catheter) into a leg artery and threads it through blood vessels to your brain using X-ray imaging.

Treatment Procedures:

  • Stereotactic radiosurgery (SRS). This treatment uses precisely focused radiation to destroy the AVM. It is not surgery in the literal sense because there is no incision.

    Instead, SRS directs many highly targeted radiation beams at the AVM to damage the blood vessels and cause scarring. The scarred AVM blood vessels then slowly clot off in one to three years following treatment.

    One example of this is the Gamma Knife

Most brain AVM are diagnosed after it ruptured so it is best to consult your doctor for proper treatment of AVM.

Life is hard. And it was harder for some.But there are those who suffered and came out to be an inspiration. One of these people is this lady, named Zaida Estavillo.

Princess Oblan

Princess Oblan, 24, wife to Ryan, was three months pregnant when she was diagnosed with Brain Arteriovenous Malformation (AVM). On May 5, 2019, Princess was rushed to the hospital after passing out. Her family found her lying on the floor unconscious.

Aaron Kurt

Aaron Kurt, 16, was misdiagnosed with Urinary Tract Infection, revived to life again, until a CT Scan of his brain showed a ruptured vessel.

Chloe Grace

Brain Arteriovenous Malformation (AVM) and Aneurysms are mainly the causes of stroke in children or young people. Chloe Grace, one of the youngest members of AVM Philippines, was only five years old when diagnosed with a ruptured Brain AVM on her brainstem. On July 8, 2018, Chloe was rushed to the hospital after their family had lunch. She had a severe headache, she was vomiting uncontrollably, and she was unable to move the left side of her body.

Debra Mier

Debra Mier, who had already experienced six (6) ruptures of Brain AVM, has chosen not to undergo surgery to remove it. Debra experienced her first attack when she was third year high school. The attack was probably a rupture or stroke that had caused her to be paralyzed for three (3) months - unable to walk or eat by herself. But because there were no hospitals equipped for her condition nearby, she was not given a proper diagnosis and the cause was not known to them.
